How the house works

Room-by-room operating notes. Use these before calling, unless there is an emergency.

Heating & cooling

Three house zones

  • First floor Nest near the back door
  • Second floor Nest in the hallway
  • Bathroom heat zone
  • First-floor AC from Fujitsu mini-split under the stairs
Mini-split remotes live in wall holders near thermostats.
Wood stove

Open the flue first

  • Use only in heating season
  • Keep screen and grate in place when door is removed
  • Never leave a fire unattended
  • Close flue only after embers are completely cold
Doors & shades

Raise shades before opening

  • Steel glass doors release at top and bottom
  • Open outward
  • Anchor rods drop into patio holes
  • Do not operate doors with shades lowered
TV & audio

Savant, Apple TV and Sonos

  • TV control is through the universal remote
  • Sonos works best on the house Wi-Fi
  • Choose rooms inside the Sonos app
  • Call Ambiance for programming or networking
Kitchen

Premium appliance setup

Fisher & Paykel refrigerator, Wolf range and oven, dishwasher, microwave, Vitamix and other small appliances.

For appliance service, start with Earl B. Feiden.
Internet

Spectrum + Ubiquiti network

The house has indoor and outdoor Wi-Fi coverage. Ambiance supports the network hardware.

Heating

Radiant floor zone

The studio floor heat is controlled by the Nest near the bathroom/laundry door.

Cooling

Fujitsu mini-split

The studio mini-split is in the vaulted ceiling above the bookcase. Point the remote directly at it.

Laundry

Stacked washer and dryer

Empty the dryer lint trap after every load. A folding drying rack and ironing setup are in the laundry area.

Doors

Steel-framed glass doors

They operate like the main house doors. Raise shades first, release top and bottom, then anchor open.

Bathroom

Studio full bath

Shower-only bath. Outdoor shower behind the studio is seasonal.

Media

TV and sound

Uses the same Savant/Sonos ecosystem as the main house.

Pool system

Saline, heated gunite pool

Filter pump and heater are normally automated. Do not make manual equipment changes unless directed by Pool Science.

Normal schedule

8 AM to 8 PM

Historic instructions set the pump/heater schedule to operate during daytime hours, with a target temperature around 80°F.

Service

Pool Science

Errol handles regular cleaning, opening, closing, heater issues, leaks and equipment repairs.

Call 845-417-8594
Safety

Fence and gate

Keep access points secured. Do not leave children or guests unattended around the pool.

Towels

Use pool towels outside

Keep wet towels and suits off wood floors and furniture.

Weather

Storm preparation

Lower and tie all umbrellas. Remove loose items from the pool area before wind or severe weather.

Propane

Underground Suburban tank

The underground tank remains Suburban Propane property. Only Suburban should service, alter, fill or remove its equipment.

Generator

Automatic whole-house backup

It should start automatically during a utility outage and stop after power is restored.

If it fails to start, call Gen Plus.
Radon

Active radon system

Test every two years and check that the system indicator remains in its normal range.
